Raiding process

Preparation

  • Gain complete awareness of encounter mechanics
  • Ensure fully enchanted gear
  • Bring sufficient raid buff consumables (food, flasks, potions, runes)

Execution

  • Listen to the the instructions of the raid leader and your role officer
  • Minimize taking avoidable damage
  • Ask questions and call out for support when needed

Review

  • Dedicate 30min of your week to review logs with your role officer
  • Target one achievable improvement goal before next raid lockout
  • Push to achieve said goal, and ask for assistance if needed

Attendance requirement

  • Maintain an overall attendance record of above 85%
  • Extended inactivity should come with a few weeks pre-warning, when possible
  • Falling under the 85% threshold will result in benching, no exceptions
  • Falling under 85% when benched will result in temporary demotion out of the raid group

Raid bench

  • Every mythic raid night a bench of 3 raiders need to be available during the entire duration of the raid.
  • Each benched player needs to be available to join the raid on a moment's notice, fully prepared.
  • Attending raids on the bench counts towards your attendance and will reward you with equal loot priority as first-team raiders when coming off the bench.
  • Who sits on the bench and who joins the first team is determined by the Raid Leader (Rowfire).
  • All bench players are guaranteed to get rotated every other week in order to ensure they get kills on the bosses they need loot from the most.

Loot council rules

Why have a loot council? So the players who contribute to raids in terms of good performance and attitude get a priority on loot over players who do not, thus creating a bit of healthy competition between us.

  • 3-player council: Rowfire, Winterbark, Ancalagon
  • Priority on loot given to high-parsing players across all specialisations.
  • All loot council members have good knowledge of what loot would suit a particular class/spec (and have a stat priority sheet on-hand to refer to)

General rules

  • Pugging of progress raids is not allowed for ANYONE, under NO CIRCUMSTANCE.
  • Pugging of farm raids is allowed only on the last day of the lockout (Tuesday) or with an individual permission from an officer.
  • Bad manners and toxic behaviour that damage the guild's image are forbidden in public.
  • Open conversation about concerns, complaints and other drama is encouraged, when kept reasonable
  • Obligatory /bow whenever you meet our glorious Queen Winterbark anywhere in the open world.
